Best Player Of The 2000's

Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant is the best player to play in the decade from 2000-2010. Although Kobe Bryant is really in the talk of greatest NBA player of all time, he is the greatest player during this time period. Kobe Bryant's success peaked in his 2005-2006 season where he averaged about 35 points per game. During this decade, Kobe won 5 NBA titles. The years that he won were 2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, and 2010. Kobe was able to win NBA Finals MVP in 2 out of those 5 championship series, in 2009 and 2010.

In his career, Kobe Bryant scored 33,643 points, averaging 25 points per game, 7,047 rebounds with a 5.2 average per game, 6,306 assists, averaging 4.7 assists per game, 1,944 steals, 1.4 steals per game, 640 total blocks, and a field goal percentage of 44.7%. Kobe’s accolades consisted of 5 NBA Championships, 2 time NBA Finals MVP, 1 time NBA MVP, 18 time All Star, 2 time NBA All Star MVP, and NBA Dunk Contest Champion.

Kobe Bryant is most known for his “mamba mentality” and ability to hit a mid range shot like no other. When you shoot a piece of balled up paper into a recycling bin, what do you say? “KOBE!”. This just shows you how often his jump shot went through the net. Kobe Bryant is debatably the greatest mid range shooter of all time. Kobe never backed down and fought through diversity in order to be as successful as he was.
