Best Player Of The 1990's

Michael Jordan

Michael Jordan is the best player to play in the decade from 1990-2000. Most people would even say Michael Jordan is the greatest player to ever step foot on a basketball court. During this decade MJ racked up stats and more importantly he racked up rings. Michael Jordan and the Bulls won the NBA championship in 1991, 1992, 1993, 1996, 1997, and 1998. Every single one of these championships resulted in a Michael Jordan finals MVP. Although he was only in the league for 15 seasons, he had won 6 finals, making him one of the winningest basketball players of all time.

In his career, Michael Jordan scored 32,292 points, averaging 30.1 points per game, 6,672 total rebounds, averaging out to 6.2 per game, 5,633 total assists with a 5.3 average per game, 2,514 steals, averaging 2.3 per game, 893 blocks, and a field goal percentage of 49.7%. Jordans accolades include being an NBA Champion 6 times, an NBA Finals MVP 6 times, a 5 time MVP, a 14 times All Star, 10 time Scoring Champion, winning Defensive Player of the Year and Rookie of the Year.
